## Have you ever experienced or witnessed great teamwork and professionalism? What made it effective?

Yes, I have experienced strong teamwork in environments where communication was open, expectations were clear, and everyone took ownership of their responsibilities. What made it effective was a 
combination of having mutal respect, clear accountability, and constant uplifting of team members. They were all very receptive to constructive feedback, and focused on solutions rather than blame. 
there was also a culture of asking questions and admitting to mistakes early on. Which created trust and transparency to collaborate effectively. 

## What steps can you take to make sure your communication is clear, respectful, and inclusive?

To ensure my communication is clear, respectful, and inclusive, I will take time to think before responding, use clear and structured language, provide enough context to avoid confusion, 
and remain mindful of my tone—especially in written communication. I will assume positive intent, ask clarifying questions rather than making assumptions, acknowledge others’ contributions, 
and be considerate of different backgrounds and communication styles so that everyone feels respected and included.

## How can you help create a positive, fun, and professional work environment?

I like to create a positive, fun, and professional work environment by being reliable, respectful, and supportive of my teammates. Maintaining a positive attitude, communicating openly, and celebrating small wins can contribute to team morale, while still meeting deadlines and taking responsibility for my work. By balancing friendliness with accountability and avoiding 
negativity or gossip, I can help foster an environment that is both enjoyable and professional.

## How can you prepare effectively for meetings with supervisors and colleagues?
I can prepare effectively for meetings with supervisors and colleagues by reviewing the agenda in advance, preparing updates on my progress, and noting any questions or blockers I need 
clarification on. Bringing relevant documents or notes ensures I can contribute meaningfully to the discussion. Being punctual, attentive, and ready to engage
shows respect for others’ time, and following up afterward on any action items helps maintain accountability and alignment.

## What would it look like to be proactive during your internship?
Being proactive during my internship would mean taking initiative rather than waiting to be directed at every step. This includes asking thoughtful questions early, identifying potential blockers before they become issues, seeking feedback regularly, and looking for ways to improve processes or contribute beyond assigned tasks. It also means managing my time effectively, staying organised, and communicating updates without being prompted. 
Overall, being proactive shows ownership, engagement, and a genuine commitment to learning and contributing to the team.

## How might you follow up with colleagues or supervisors if you need information/action from them? How would you escalate it further if you don't get a response and when would you escalate?

If I need information or action from a colleague or supervisor, I would first send a clear and polite message explaining what I need, why it’s needed, and any relevant deadlines. If I don’t receive a response within a reasonable timeframe, I would follow up 
respectfully, for example by saying, “Just checking in on this when you have a moment.” If the matter becomes urgent or is blocking progress, I would escalate by informing my 
supervisor and providing context, including what steps I’ve already taken to resolve it. Escalation should be calm, factual, and focused on the impact of the delay rather than wait.

## What are some things you won't do?

Some things I won’t do include ignoring messages or responsibilities, missing deadlines without communicating early, or reacting defensively to feedback. 
I won’t blame others instead of taking accountability for my work, speak negatively about colleagues, or share confidential information inappropriately. 
I also won’t disengage during meetings or avoid asking questions when clarification is needed. Maintaining professionalism means being responsible, 
respectful, and transparent in all interactions.
