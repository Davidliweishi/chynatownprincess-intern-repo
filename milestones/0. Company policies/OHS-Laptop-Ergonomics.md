# Ergonomic Workspace Reflection

## Research & Learn

### Risks of Using a Laptop Without External Equipment

Working directly on a laptop without an external monitor or keyboard can lead to:

- Neck strain from constantly looking down at the screen
- Rounded shoulders and poor spinal alignment
- Wrist discomfort from awkward keyboard angles
- Increased risk of repetitive strain injuries (RSI)
- Eye strain due to improper screen height and distance
- Lower back pain from slouching posture

Over time, these issues can reduce focus, productivity, and long-term physical health.

---

### Ergonomic Equipment That Improves Posture

Several tools can significantly improve workspace ergonomics:

- **Laptop stand** – Raises screen to eye level
- **External keyboard** – Keeps wrists in a neutral position
- **External mouse** – Reduces wrist strain from trackpad overuse
- **External monitor** – Allows proper head and neck alignment
- **Ergonomic chair** – Supports lower back and promotes upright posture
- **Footrest** – Helps maintain proper leg positioning

---

### Ideal Workspace Adjustments

- Monitor top should be at or slightly below eye level
- Screen should be about arm’s length away
- Shoulders relaxed, elbows at ~90°
- Wrists straight and not bent upward
- Feet flat on the floor
- Lower back supported by chair

---

### Daily Habits to Reduce Strain

- Take movement breaks every 30–60 minutes
- Follow the 20-20-20 rule for eye strain (every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds)
- Perform posture checks throughout the day
- Stretch neck, shoulders, wrists regularly
- Avoid sitting in the same position for long periods

## Reflection

### Equipment Changes I Can Make

To improve my setup, I can:

- Use a laptop stand to raise my screen to eye level
- Use an external keyboard and mouse
- Adjust my chair height so my elbows remain at 90°
- Consider using an external monitor for extended work sessions

These changes would reduce neck strain and improve overall posture.

---

### Behavioural Changes I Can Implement

- Sit upright with shoulders relaxed
- Avoid leaning forward toward the screen
- Take regular standing or walking breaks
- Stretch wrists and shoulders throughout the day
- Keep both feet flat on the ground

---

### Reminder System

To maintain consistency, I can use **Focus Bear** to schedule:

- Hourly movement reminders
- Posture check alerts
- Short stretch sessions during work blocks

Using structured reminders will help turn good posture into a daily habit rather than something I forget about.

---

## Workspace Adjustment Implemented

I raised my laptop using a stand and adjusted my chair height so my elbows remain at a 90° angle while typing. I also enabled movement reminders during a full workday.

### Observed Improvements

- Reduced neck strain
- Less shoulder tension
- Improved awareness of posture
- Better concentration after movement breaks

Even small ergonomic changes noticeably improved comfort and productivity.
