## When should you use AI for assistance, and when should you rely on your own skills?

You should use AI tools when you are need to streamline, brainstorm or explain something to better your understanding. However, you should not rely on it to create something entirely without
your own effort. Therefore, you should rely on your own skills, when there is need for critical thinking, creativity and understanding foundational topics in coding, 
etc. 

## How can you avoid over-reliance on AI while still benefiting from it?

You should see AI as a tool, rather than a replacement for your work and judgement. We must manually attack a task first, and try to generate and create your own solutions
to a problem first. Another good way to avoid over-reliance is doing a percentage balance of AI/personal effort. For example, 80%/20% - 80% should be your own personal effort and 
ideas, and 20% should be of assistance, notes and other contributions from AI. 

## What steps will you take to ensure data privacy when using AI tools?

- Never share sensitive personal/organisation information on AI tools (passwords, application/project specific codes, bank details,etc)
- Use Mock/Synthetic data: this is especially true if you are brainstorming or testing/training models.
- Review Legal and company policies to remind yourself of what you can and cannot put into AI tools.
- Disable data training (if possible): You can disable an AI tool's ability to train future models with your information.
- Regularly delete your chat and data history, to stop data retention.
