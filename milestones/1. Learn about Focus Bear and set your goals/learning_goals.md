📌 What Do You Want to Learn & Contribute?
✅ Reflection Questions
📌 Take some time to reflect on what you want to gain from this internship and how you can contribute. Answer the following questions:

What do you hope to learn from this internship?

I'd like to learn what it actually feels like to contribute to a real product that real users depend on. At uni, projects have a clear start and end, but I want to experience the messier, ongoing nature of real software development — how teams prioritise features, handle bugs, and make technical decisions under constraints.

Since I don't have backend experience, I'm also hoping to see that side of things — even just understanding how the frontend and backend connect will make me a more well-rounded developer. Mostly though, I want to find out where my gaps actually are, because I think you only discover those by doing the real thing.

What skills are you most excited to develop?

Javascript is a skill I'm most eager to further deepen. I have a basic foundation, but it's been mostly used in isolation - small personal projects, coding exercises and uni projects with no real stakes. I'm excited to see how it is used in a real setting, where code needs to be accessible and readable to other programmers. 

I'm also curious about Nest.js. Having no experience in dealing with it, gaining even basic exposure to see how APIs are structured and how data flows between the frontend and backend would fill a gap I know I currently have.

I'd love to further improve my debugging skills and learn Node.js. I currently have developed coding skills in uni. Hence seeing it in a real context and actively engaging with it would help streamline streamline my understanding and learn it quicker. This is better than learning in an isolated environment like a classroom. 

What do you think you can contribute to the team?

While my technical skills are still developing, I think I can contribute a fresh perspective and a genuine willingness to do the unglamorous work — whether that's writing documentation, testing features, or picking up tasks that more senior developers don't have time for.

What is one area you feel less confident in that you’d like to improve?

Debugging is an area I want to get significantly better at. Right now when something breaks, I can find the issue eventually — but my process is more trial-and-error than systematic. I don't yet have the instinct to read an error, form a hypothesis, and trace it efficiently.

I think working in a real codebase will force me to develop that skill faster than anything else, because the problems are messier and the stakes are higher than anything I've encountered in uni. I'm hoping to pick up better habits around reading stack traces, using debugging tools properly, and learning how experienced developers approach a bug they've never seen before.

What does success in this internship look like for you?

Success for me would mean finishing this internship having made a real, visible contribution — not just observing or completing work, but actually creating something that  makes a difference. 

Personally - I'd consider it a success if I leave with a clearer sense of where I stand as a developer — what I'm capable of, where my gaps are, and what kind of engineer I want to become. I don't expect to be an expert by the end, but I do want to feel like I meaningfully levelled up.

I'd also consider it a success if the team felt I was reliable — someone who asked good questions and can communicate clearly.
