## What is Agile, and how does it differ from traditional project management?

Agile is considered a flexible project management framework - focused on continious improvement, collaboration and delviering small, function tasks of a project called sprints. In traditional (waterfall) management, it prioritises delivering a complete final product near the end, has a rigid structure, and has hierarchical, defined roles within the working project. It also involves customers only at the  start and finish - whereas Agile has constant customer involvemen throughout. 

## What are the core values and principles of Agile? (Hint: Check the Agile Manifesto.)

4 core values:

1) Team communicatio and collaboration is preferred over rigid workflows or software tools.
2) Delivering a working, functional software rather than software that requires constant maintainece and extensive documentation. 
3) Constant customer collaboration, rather than focusing on contract negotiations and obligations.
4) Encourages flexibility when change happens, rather than follow a plan or sticking to a rigid plan. 

## What are the differences between Scrum and Kanban, and when is each used?

Scrum uses short bursts of productive sessions (sprints) with defined roles (e.g Scrum master, Product owner, etc.) to deliver incremented, high-value work. It is typically used to break down complex, large projects into managable chunk sizes - allowing for immediate feedback and minimise any risk. It is often felixible due to the volatile nature of project delveopments, where anything can change or happen. 

Kanban focuses on continious workflow, and work-in-progress (WIP) - which are a limited number of tasks allowed in specific project stages, preventing multitasking. This is used in active workflows like development an testing stages. 

## Why does Focus Bear lean towards Kanban instead of Scrum?

Focus Bear's work is very much focusd on helping people with ADHD to complete work. Therefore, Kanban's continious workflow fulfills this philosophy. Several specific Kanban features align this:

1) The visual interface of Kanban helps people to grasp an entire workflow at a glance, reducing any potential visual/cognative overload.
2) Its continiuous workflow model allows users to adjust based on priorities and workload.
3) Kanban has a limit to how many "WIP" (work in progress) you can do. Therefore, requiring you to focus on a task at hand.
4) Rather than a time-constrained sprint, Kanban allows users to monitor their rate of progress in the total amount of time completing a taks - removing the anxiety of compelting something before time runs out.

## What do you think are the biggest benefits and challenges of Agile?

Benefits:
1) Adaptability - the ability to adapt your workflow means you can constantly check up on your work without being locked into any hard requirements.
2) Quick feedback - faster feedback loops means any bugs and other issues are resolved earlier on, rather than later.
3) User autonomy - Developers can self-organise their workflow - which allows them to take personal ownership.
4) Customer collaboration - customer is there constantly in collab, rather than at the start and end.

Challenges:

1) Poorly defined roles - easier to misunderstand roles and responsibilities (e.g Scrum master), leading to confusion over who is doing what.
2) Undefined time scope - with no rigid, up-front plan, projects can sometimes lose track fo what needs to be done by when, making it hard to estimate project completion.
3) High time commitment: daily standups, continious collaboration and constant feedback loops can lead to excessive meetings and developer burn out. Perhaps even unnecessary time wasted.
